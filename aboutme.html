About Me

My name is Soumeng Chea, and I am an aspiring Boston-based UX/UI Researcher and Design. I am currently a second-year MS in Computer Science candidate at Northeastern University, focusing on Computer-Human Interaction.

Before my Computer Science journey, I graduated from the University of Massachusetts Lowell with a bachelor of art in Political Science and a minor in French. Now, you wonder why I decided to pursue a Computer Science career instead of going for any other social science graduate program. Well, I see computer science education as the art of thinking about problems using logic, pattern recognition, and sub-division into smaller issues. The knowledge would allow me to write software programs that make computers to accomplish tasks more efficiently. I believe a computer science degree's versatility will allow me to use my social science problem-solving skills to deal with obstacles from a different perspective. My interest in computer science comes from my view of the discipline as a challenging and creative topic. I am eager to study the technological foundation of software engineering and the idea of interaction between computers and the user resemblance of human-to-human, open-ended dialogue.

Outside of the academic setting, I am a huge cooker and baker – I find both activities very therapeutic. Besides cooking and baking, I also love to try new restaurants and new foods. A traveler – I had lived in Laos for four years before my family migrated to the United States. My next traveling idea is to make backpacking trips to East and Southeast Asia countries alone. I currently spoke four languages and would love to learn more – I am advanced in oral proficiency in Khmer, Laotian, and Thai. At the same time, my French is only at an intermediate level in verbal, reading, and writing.

Now, you're wondering why I decided to have a webpage address as icodealittle. The answer is simple: I honestly don't have an answer to that. I just thought the username is a fun way to describe myself in a new industry.
